ABOUT THE FIRM

About the firm
Gazi & Associates is a commercial law firm advising clients on tax, corporate, regulatory and litigation matters in Bangladesh. The firm was established in 2014 and operates from chambers in Dhaka, Jashore and London.
The firm's work is concentrated in three areas: advising businesses on their tax and regulatory obligations to the Bangladesh state; representing clients in disputes with the National Board of Revenue and before the High Court; and providing ongoing corporate counsel to companies operating in or entering the Bangladesh market.
Clients include Bangladeshi private companies, foreign-owned subsidiaries, family-owned groups, and individual professionals and entrepreneurs. The firm acts for clients in industries including manufacturing, textiles, logistics, technology and professional services, and maintains a particular focus on the UK–Bangladesh corridor through its London chamber.
How the firm works
The firm is structured around the principle that legal work in tax and regulatory matters is fundamentally advisory rather than transactional. Each matter is led by a senior lawyer and resourced by associates with specific expertise in the relevant area, whether before the NBR, the company registrar, the customs authorities or the courts.
The firm does not take on every matter that is offered to it. It accepts work where it can act effectively, where there is no conflict, and where the engagement is structured to produce a clear outcome for the client. Fees are agreed in writing at the outset of every engagement.
Regulatory affiliations
The principal of the firm is an Advocate of the Supreme Court of Bangladesh, a registered Income Tax Practitioner under the National Board of Revenue, and a member of the Bangladesh Bar Council, Dhaka Bar Association and Dhaka Taxes Bar Association.
Chambers
The firm's Dhaka chamber, in Purana Paltan, is the principal office and the base for the firm's tax, corporate and litigation work. The Jashore chamber serves the firm's clients in the industrial and port belt around Noapara, with a particular concentration of work for manufacturing and logistics clients. The London chamber, in Ilford, supports the firm's UK-based clients on Bangladesh-related matters and provides a point of contact for diaspora and inbound investment work.

PRACTICE AREA — TAX & NBR ADVISORY

Tax & NBR Advisory
The firm advises corporate and individual clients on the full range of Bangladesh tax matters, from routine compliance to contested proceedings before the National Board of Revenue and the courts.
Tax work in Bangladesh is shaped by the Income Tax Act 2023, the annual Finance Act, and the published and unpublished practice of the NBR. The firm's tax practice is built on direct experience with that practice — how returns are read, how audits are opened, what arguments hold weight at appeal, and how the relevant tribunals approach the questions that recur in this jurisdiction.
The firm acts for Bangladeshi private companies, foreign-owned subsidiaries, partnerships, and high-earning individuals including non-residents with Bangladesh-source income.
How the firm advises
The firm's tax work is organised in four streams:
Advisory and planning. Structuring transactions, group reorganisations, employment arrangements and inbound investment to produce predictable and lawful tax outcomes. This includes review of proposed transactions before they are executed, and ongoing advice to in-house finance teams on the tax treatment of specific arrangements.
Compliance. Preparation and review of corporate and individual returns, withholding obligations, and the supporting documentation that determines whether a return survives scrutiny. The firm's view is that the work done at the return stage determines what is possible at the audit stage; compliance is treated accordingly.
Audits and assessments. Representation in NBR audits and assessment proceedings, including drafting of responses, preparation of supporting evidence, and conduct of hearings before the relevant Deputy Commissioner of Taxes and Joint Commissioner of Taxes.
Appeals and litigation. Appeals to the Commissioner of Taxes (Appeals), references to the Taxes Appellate Tribunal, and tax-related writ and reference jurisdiction matters before the High Court Division of the Supreme Court of Bangladesh.
The firm also advises on transfer pricing documentation and disputes, double taxation treaty positions (including under the UK–Bangladesh treaty), and the tax aspects of cross-border employment and secondment.
